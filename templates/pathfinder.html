We Are The Powerful Youthful Mighty Pathfinders.

Definition

The Pathfinder club is the second club in the Adventist Youth Movement.

"Pathfindering" conjures up images of marching, camping, curious sports, bugs and bats for those who are familiar with it. Broadening their eyes on the world and building a relationship with God are the twin goals of this group, designed for children aged 10-15 years. Of nearly 2 million members worldwide, this group funded by the Seventh-day Adventist Church welcomes any youth who agrees to abide by the Pathfinder Pledge and Law irrespective of their church affiliations.

Leadership

Volunteer leadership, capable of numerous excellent skills and interests, is, above all, firm faithful to Jesus Christ and knows the path they follow to eternal life. We make the Pathfinder club a laboratory of exploration in which development and learning are a synonym for fun. Not a failure is predicted, but all tasks are a learning tool.

Activities

The creativity of the leadership and members of a club is the key limiting factor. Activities support a variety of initiatives in their community and around the world, including research on nature and the environment, hiking and high-adventure excursions. Walking tests each member's unique talents. Pathfindering is based on an old-fashioned six level program with about 350 specialized areas for art and crafts, water, nature, household arts, leisure, spiritual development, health and professional education. These are often the starting point for professions or hobbies in life.

Why Achievement Classes?

Years ago when your fathers and mothers were boys and girls, a spirit of prophecy called you to unite all young people together to help complete the task of bringing Advent to the world in this century. The Adventist Youth Society was formed in response to these and other similar messages. And so the years went by, and boys and girls from all over the world came together and prayed and studied and went out to work. A huge job was done, and the once small band has increased to thousands of people and spread around the globe.

But how did Pathfinder lessons come into being? When the boys and girls met and worked and became involved in AY / AJY events, the leaders felt the need to provide more ways to help them to develop a strong Christian character in order to better understand how to serve God when they are needed. Several suggestions were found in the writings of the Spirit of Prophetry during the earnest analysis given to the issue and some of them are cited here for your inspiration and encouragement:

“The children are to be trained to become missionaries; they must be helped to understand distinctly what they must do to be saved.” {Counsels to Parents, Teachers, and Students, page 168}. And the best preparation “is the harmonious development of the physical, the mental, and the spiritual powers. It prepares the student for the joy of service in this world and for the higher joy of wider service in the world to come.” {Education, page 13}.

Uniform

The standard Pathfinder helps to make the Pathfinder programme. This reflects the values and principles of the worldwide community. Any member of this association becomes a very important representative of the group, and the uniform should help to make the club aware of its identity, which the adventist youth of today rightly reflects. This fails to achieve its function if the uniform is worn like ordinary clothes.
